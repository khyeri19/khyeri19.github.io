Welcome to my portfolio

Grammar check: I am a bachelor's student majoring in economics and mathematics with a minor in statistics. This portfolio showcases my work from the summer course UCSCIEARL5, focusing on Geographic Information Systems (GIS). GIS is a computerized tool that captures, stores, analyzes, and presents geographically referenced data. It integrates hardware, software, data, and methods to handle and examine various types of spatial information. Essentially, GIS allows users to comprehend, interpret, and visualize patterns and connections within geographic data. It enables users to generate, modify, and manipulate spatial data layers, such as maps, satellite images, aerial photos, and survey data. These layers represent diverse features such as land elevations, roads, rivers, buildings, vegetation, and more. Throughout the course, I acquired the following knowledge and skills:

  1. Basics of GIS
  2. Data and Spatial Analysis
    1. Data cleaning techniques
    2. Different data types: vector or raster
  3. Creation of different types of maps: Choropleth map, Interactive map, and Web map
  4. Utilization of various software tools: ArcGIS, QGIS, and Google Earth Engine
  5. Development of Mobile Apps
